Introduction

“Hyper-attrition” has plagued HR management for a number of different occupations, including for local package delivery people and software developers. In the HR analytics space, there is significant activity around building products that help companies predict who is likely to leave and when. Such tools can help considerably when engaging in future workforce planning or by allowing managers to take steps ahead of time to retain high-performers.

This lab asks you to build a machine learning based prediction tool and evaluate it in terms of potential for perpetuating algorithmic bias in promotion decisions, a subject we will talk about in more detail in the second half of the semester . It is recommended that most of the work for the lab be done in WEKA. You may need to use Excel (or if you prefer, R or Python) to answer a few simple cross-tabulation related questions in the assignment. For submission, create a new document with your name and answers to each of the questions in the assignment.
